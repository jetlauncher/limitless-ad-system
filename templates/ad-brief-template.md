# Ad Campaign Brief

Fill in this template before generating ad creatives. The more specific you are, the better your output will be.

---

## Campaign Overview

**Product / Offer:**
> What are you selling? Be specific — include the format, duration, price point, and delivery method if relevant.

**Event or Deadline (if applicable):**
> e.g. "Workshop on [date] in [city]" or "Limited enrollment — 30 seats"

**Goal of This Campaign:**
> What do you want someone to do after seeing the ad?
> e.g. "Click to register", "DM for details", "Book a free call"

---

## Target Audience

**Who is this for?**
> Job title, industry, or life stage. Be specific.
> e.g. "Non-technical business owners aged 35-55 running teams of 5-50 people in Bangkok"

**What do they want?**
> Their aspirations — what outcome are they chasing?

**What is stopping them?**
> The real obstacles: fear, confusion, past failures, cost concerns, time pressure.

**What do they already believe?**
> Their existing worldview. What assumptions do they carry in?

---

## The Offer

**What's included:**
> List the deliverables or outcomes. What do they get?

**What makes it different:**
> Why this, why now, why from you?

**Price / Investment:**
> The actual amount and what it gets them.

**Guarantee or risk reversal (if any):**
> e.g. "If you don't build at least one working agent, we'll refund you"

---

## Proof Points

**Social proof:**
> Testimonials, student results, logos, or numbers you can reference.

**Your authority:**
> Why are you the right person to teach or deliver this?

**Track record:**
> Past cohorts, clients, outcomes, or case studies.

---

## Messaging Angles to Test

List the angles you want to explore. Aim for at least 5.

- [ ] **Pain** — Lead with the problem they're living right now
- [ ] **Mechanism** — Explain how the solution works differently
- [ ] **Offer** — Lead with what they get and why it's worth it
- [ ] **Urgency** — Time or scarcity pressure
- [ ] **Objection Handling** — Address the #1 reason they wouldn't buy
- [ ] **Social Proof** — Lead with a result or transformation
- [ ] **Reframe** — Challenge a belief they currently hold
- [ ] **Curiosity / Awareness** — Introduce an idea they haven't considered
- [ ] **Founder Authority** — Lead with your story or credibility
- [ ] **Outcome** — Paint the picture of life after the transformation

## Creative Format Notes

**Visual style:**
> e.g. "Clean and minimal", "Bold text on dark background", "Before/after split"

**Copy length:**
> e.g. "Short hooks only (under 8 words)" or "Long-form with full argument"

**Tone:**
> e.g. "Direct and confident", "Warm and educational", "Provocative"

**Languages:**
> e.g. "Thai primary, English secondary" or "English only"

- e.g. Don't make it look like a generic tech ad
