# Brand Book Review Checklist

Use this when you have a client brand book (or your own) and you want to make sure you've extracted everything Claude needs to generate on-brand creative.

Work through this checklist before starting a generation session. Check off what you have, note what's missing.

---

## Brand Foundation

- [ ] Brand name and tagline documented
- [ ] Mission statement or brand purpose
- [ ] Brand values (usually 3-5)
- [ ] Target audience description
- [ ] Positioning statement (who you're for, what you do, why you're different)
- [ ] Competitor landscape noted

---

## Voice and Tone

- [ ] Tone of voice guidelines (formal/casual, warm/direct, etc.)
- [ ] Do/Don't word lists or phrasing examples
- [ ] Sample copy that represents the brand voice
- [ ] Specific words to use
- [ ] Specific words to avoid
- [ ] Grammar and style preferences (e.g. Oxford comma, sentence case, etc.)

---

## Visual Identity

- [ ] Primary logo (and clear space rules)
- [ ] Secondary logos or wordmark variants
- [ ] Colour palette with hex/CMYK/RGB codes
- [ ] Typography — primary and secondary typefaces
- [ ] Photography style guidelines
- [ ] Illustration style (if applicable)
- [ ] Icon style
- [ ] Pattern or texture assets
- [ ] Layout and grid principles

---

## Advertising and Campaign Guidelines

- [ ] Approved ad formats or templates
- [ ] Headline style guidance
- [ ] CTA preferences
- [ ] Legal or compliance disclaimers required
- [ ] Endorsement or partnership rules
- [ ] Examples of approved ads
- [ ] Examples of rejected or off-brand ads

---

## What to Extract for Claude

After reviewing the brand book, pull out the following and save to `brand/generated-briefs/brand-brief.md`:

1. **Brand voice summary** — 3-5 sentences describing how the brand sounds
2. **Visual style summary** — 3-5 sentences describing how the brand looks
3. **Copy rules** — bullet list of dos and don'ts
4. **Key messages** — the 3-5 things this brand always says
5. **Forbidden directions** — what this brand never does visually or verbally
6. **Audience profile** — who they are and what they care about
7. **CTA style** — how the brand asks for action
